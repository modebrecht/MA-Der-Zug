## 0. Function of this document

This document defines the novel's red thread:

- act structure,
- chapter order,
- disappearance order,
- information distribution,
- motif planting and payoff,
- emotional escalation,
- hidden-truth protection for Levin,
- transition seams between acts.

It is not prose. It is the structural route from which each chapter receives a beat plan under `05-workflow.md`.

---

## 1. Structure decision

The novel is planned as **20 chapters**.

| Act | Chapters | Function | Main question |
|---|---:|---|---|
| **Act I — Stillstand** | 1–6 | train, ensemble, first Tilgung, objective world-break | Is Levin wrong — or is the world wrong? |
| **Act II — Spuren** | 7–11 | relational Tilgung, traces, first historical shape | What connects these people? |
| **Midpoint** | 11 | Albula 2008 becomes visible | The train is moving through the accident. |
| **Act III — Schuldketten** | 12–17 | individual guilt mirrors, Marlene / Robert / Selma / Hediger payoffs | What did each person do, avoid or fail to say? |
| **Act IV — 02:47** | 18–20 | Levin alone with form, Führerstand, braking, Sarah | What does Levin do when the truth names him? |

### Target size

- Target: **70,000–80,000 words**.
- Recommended ideal: **ca. 75,000 words / ca. 300 pages**.
- Average chapter: roughly **3,500–4,000 words**, with shorter pressure chapters and longer reveal chapters as needed.

### Why 20 chapters

- Act I gets enough space for atmosphere, ensemble and first rule-showing.
- The Midpoint lands on Chapter 11, slightly after the mathematical middle, because Act I deliberately starts slow and dense.
- Act IV remains short and hard. The finale should not explain; it should execute.

## 2. Red-thread escalation

```text
impossible train
→ damaged traces
→ Albula 2008
→ individual guilt-chains
→ Levin's form
→ braking
→ Sarah
```

### Act I — Stillstand

The reader learns the train as a real place. Levin travels from the Engadin toward Chur to see Sarah. The train stops. Tobias Reck is established through Platz 47 and erased. Only Levin remembers. Marlene becomes a possible perception-counterpart. The act ends with an objective world-break: the train stands still, but snow / time / the outside behave as if something is still moving.

### Act II — Spuren

The Tilgung repeats, but changes layer. Jan disappears from Anika's history. Hediger gives first Albula fragments. Robert becomes a sleeping truth-carrier. Marlene and Levin grow closer through observation, not romance. At the Midpoint, Levin understands: the relevant passengers are tied to the Albula accident of 2008.

### Act III — Schuldketten

The shared accident becomes individual guilt. Anika / Jan, Robert, Selma / Eliah / Linn, Hediger and Marlene each mirror a different way of not stopping the chain. Marlene remembers she was the driver and later disappears / goes in recognition. Levin remains, but his own role is still not consciously named.

### Act IV — 02:47

After Marlene's absence, Levin finds the concrete proof: form, signature, `in Ordnung`, 00:48, old RhB self. The train reaches 02:47. Mancini gives a possibility, not a lecture. Levin brakes. The past remains. The repression ends. Final location: **Café in Zürich** with Sarah. Snow falls downward.

---

## 3. Disappearance / release order

Disappearance is not a slasher order. Each absence must damage a different layer of reality.

| Order | Figure | Type | Function |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Tobias Reck** | hard Tilgung | memory, seat, ticket, luggage and social certainty can be rewritten |
| 2 | **Jan** | relational Tilgung | a person can be erased from a shared life |
| 3 | **Robert Marek** | delayed awakening + Tilgung | not telling public truth is active guilt |
| 4 | **Selma / Linn strand** | quiet release / wound recognition | the accident becomes human, not numerical |
| 5 | **Frau Hediger** | conscious going / release | remembering alone is not yet freedom |
| 6 | **Marlene** | recognition + loss | professional guilt and emotional counterpart paid off before Levin |
| 7 | **Levin** | last unresolved center | his repressed responsibility holds the train until 02:47 |

### No-gos

- No disappearance as cheap punishment.
- No splatter / monster-train effect.
- No figure disappears before serving their mirror-function for Levin.
- No afterlife / purgatory framing.

## ACT I — STILLSTAND

### Chapter 1 — Der letzte Zug

**Location / time:** evening / night, Engadin departure toward Albula, train still normal.  
**Surface:** Levin boards after a professional appointment. He wants to reach Chur to see Sarah. He is tired, controlled, too deep in files and messages.  
**Figures:** Levin, first sight of Marlene, Tobias, Hediger, Anika / Jan, Selma / Eliah, Mancini. Robert only hinted as sleeping in the rear.  
**Must do:** establish route logic: Levin is not coming directly from Zürich, but from the Engadin. Plant Sarah as present-world anchor.  
**Hidden-truth seeds:** 02:47 as small clock / phone / display irritation; metallic splitting in half-sleep; aborted RhB / summer-job thought.  
**Motif:** cold glass, Sarah message, first wrong sound.  
**End:** the train enters darkness / tunnel. Levin feels he has heard the sound before.

### Chapter 2 — Halt

**Location / time:** night, Albula line, train stopped in snow.  
**Surface:** power / signal / announcements fail. Passengers react realistically: annoyance, tiredness, rational explanations.  
**Figures:** Tobias takes first interpretive authority; Marlene observes; Hediger stays too calm; Mancini is polite but evasive.  
**Must do:** establish external threat without explaining mystery.  
**Hidden-truth seeds:** Levin knows railway processes too precisely. `Weiche`, `Störung` or `Wartung` hits him physically.  
**Motif:** emergency light, cold, snow.  
**End:** a display jumps to / sticks at 02:47 although that cannot be correct.

### Chapter 3 — Platz 47

**Location / time:** Levin's carriage and Speisewagen, night / early morning.  
**Surface:** Tobias Reck is made concrete at Platz 47: voice, coffee ring, luggage, ticket, one sentence about liability / process.  
**Figures:** Tobias against Levin; Marlene observes; Mancini checks presence more than tickets.  
**Must do:** Tobias must feel real before disappearing.  
**Hidden-truth seeds:** Tobias uses process language; Levin reacts to `Abnahme`, `Protokoll`, `in Ordnung`.  
**Motif:** Platz 47, cold coffee, ticket.  
**End:** Levin does not consciously mark Tobias as important, but one detail stays.

### Chapter 4 — Marlenes Kamera

**Location / time:** Speisewagen / window area.  
**Surface:** Levin and Marlene speak longer for the first time. No romance; adult, careful connection through observation.  
**Figures:** Marlene with mechanical camera; she takes an image that later becomes damaged.  
**Must do:** plant Marlene as perception-counterpart and camera as broken memory instrument.  
**Hidden-truth seeds:** Marlene reacts with too much body-knowledge to brakes / tunnel / driver-near space. Levin notices but does not decode.  
**Motif:** camera, negative, window reflection.  
**End:** Marlene says in meaning, not fixed prose: sometimes something is missing before you know what.

### Chapter 5 — Der leere Platz

**Location / time:** morning in the standing train.  
**Surface:** Tobias is gone. Nobody remembers. Platz 47 is empty or was supposedly never occupied. His bag, ticket, digital trace and conversation traces have adapted.  
**Figures:** Levin against the group's reality; Marlene takes the gap more seriously; Hediger observes Levin.  
**Must do:** first full Tilgung: person, memory, material traces, social certainty.  
**What Levin knows:** he remembers Tobias.  
**What Levin does not know:** why Tobias.  
**Residue:** coffee ring, sentence fragment, Levin note, photo gap or wrong timestamp.  
**Motif:** Platz 47, coffee ring, damaged photo trace.  
**End:** Levin must accept that it is not only a missing man, but a missing reality.

### Chapter 6 — Schnee, der nicht fällt

**Location / time:** end of Act I.  
**Surface:** Levin tries to build a rational theory. Marlene checks her camera. Mancini remains too calm. Hediger corrects a small route / time detail.  
**Must do:** objective world-break.  
**Final image:** the train stands, but outside the snow moves as if the train were still travelling. A clock returns to 02:47. In the window Levin sees, for a moment, not only himself.  
**Reader may know:** the train is not normal; Levin is not simply ill.  
**Still hidden:** Albula 2008, Levin's signature, Marlene as driver.  
**End question:** If the train is not moving, why does the outside world move anyway?

## ACT II — SPUREN

### Chapter 7 — Das Foto ohne Jan

**Location / time:** Speisewagen / Anika's seating area, after Act-I world-break.  
**Surface:** Jan disappears. Anika does not remember him. A phone photo shows her alone, although Levin saw them as a pair.  
**Figures:** Anika, Jan as missing form, Levin, Marlene.  
**Must do:** second Tilgung type: relationship is rewritten.  
**Seam from Act I:** begin from Tobias residue / Platz 47. Do not reset tension.  
**Info:** Tilgung affects biographies and emotional reality, not only objects.  
**Planting:** Anika reacts to Filisur / station / smoke without understanding.  
**Motif:** couple photo becomes single-person photo.  
**End:** Levin understands that any part of a life can be deleted.

### Chapter 8 — Frau Hedigers Zeitung

**Location / time:** quieter carriage / window seat.  
**Surface:** Hediger gives Levin a first historical fragment: clipping, police photo or place-name. Not the whole truth.  
**Figures:** Hediger, Levin, Marlene. Anika should receive one brief damaged echo: wrong photo framing, `wir` → `ich`, or grief without object.  
**Must do:** bring the first Albula past into the book without firing the Midpoint too early.  
**Info:** there was an accident on this route; the location may not be random.  
**Author knowledge:** all relevant passengers are connected; Levin and Marlene especially.  
**Motif:** old paper, Landwasser / Filisur / 14 February without full explanation.  
**End:** Levin physically recognizes a detail he cannot place.

### Chapter 9 — Der hintere Wagen

**Location / time:** Wagen 5/6, dark service area, Robert asleep.  
**Surface:** Levin and Marlene search for traces or normal explanation. They reach the rear. Robert sleeps, but his objects speak: notebook, recorder, old envelope.  
**Figures:** Levin, Marlene, Robert as sleeping body, Mancini at edge. Add a quiet Selma / Eliah pre-echo if natural: a hummed line, a reaction to glass, a too-old body memory.  
**Must do:** plant Robert as truth-carrier without paying him off. Expand train map.  
**Info:** there is a suppressed story about maintenance / route / RhB.  
**Hidden-truth seed:** Levin recognizes the form of a maintenance document too quickly.  
**Motif:** recorder, envelope, sleeping body.  
**End:** an audio recording contains only metallic splitting.

### Chapter 10 — Aussteigen

**Location / time:** door / transition / snow threshold.  
**Surface:** someone tests the outside. Not an action escape, but a controlled threshold attempt. The outside behaves wrong: snow, cold, distance, direction.  
**Figures:** Levin, Marlene, Mancini, perhaps a minor passenger. Anika may receive another brief echo if Ch8 did not. Selma / Eliah should receive at least one pre-echo before Ch14 across Ch9–11.  
**Must do:** show rule: leaving is flight, not solution.  
**Info:** the train is closed not by normal doors but by meaning.  
**No-go:** no survival-action, no frozen horror sculpture as main motor.  
**Motif:** door, breath, snow, glass.  
**End:** outside seems briefly not like the current night, but another one.

### Chapter 11 — Albula 2008

**Location / time:** Speisewagen / conductor-near area / window with Landwasser hint.  
**Surface:** fragments connect: Hediger's paper, Robert trace, Anika's wound, Marlene's camera, outside wrongness, repeated 02:47.  
**MIDPOINT:** the train is not moving through space. It is moving through what connects them to 2008.  
**What Levin still does not know:** his concrete role, Marlene's role, the signature.  
**Figures:** Levin, Marlene, Hediger, Mancini; Anika remains damaged presence if needed.  
**Must do:** shift from external mystery to moral mystery.  
**Motif:** 14 February, 02:47, Landwasser, false switch as hint, no form yet.  
**End:** Levin does not say guilt. He finds the date, and his body reacts as if a door opened inside him.

## ACT III — SCHULDKETTEN

### Chapter 12 — Die Augenzeugen

**Location / time:** Anika's area / memory-pressure space.  
**Surface:** Anika begins to feel Jan as a gap, not a restored person. Her eyewitness guilt surfaces: smoke, switch-heater, station, private shame.  
**Figures:** Anika, Levin, Marlene. Jan remains erased but present as absence.  
**Must do:** show small private avoidance with large public consequence.  
**Seam from Act II:** Ch12 must pay off the Anika echoes from Ch8–11. It must not reintroduce her cold.  
**Info:** not every guilt is direct action; silence can become action.  
**Mirror for Levin:** he also wants to treat a small act as incidental.  
**Motif:** smoke, photo, missing second shadow.  
**End:** Anika cannot remember Jan, but grieves something without a name.

### Chapter 13 — Roberts Aufnahme

**Location / time:** rear carriage / Robert briefly awake or his recording decoded.  
**Surface:** Robert had hints about RhB problems and never wrote the story.  
**Figures:** Robert, Levin, Marlene.  
**Must do:** guilt through unwritten truth; sharpen Levin's historian mirror.  
**Info:** organizational faults, pressure, softened official language, records.  
**Hidden-truth seed:** a maintenance form appears as category, not Levins form.  
**Disappearance:** Robert may be erased at chapter end, quietly, almost like sleep turning final.  
**Motif:** recorder, unpublished story, sleep.  
**End:** Robert is gone; recorder contains static and one sentence fragment.

### Chapter 14 — Linns Lied

**Location / time:** Wagen 4 / quiet emotional space.  
**Surface:** Selma and Eliah move forward. Eliah remembers not cognitively but bodily: song, small hand, cold window, splitting.  
**Figures:** Selma, Eliah, Levin, Marlene.  
**Must do:** ground the accident emotionally. Fourteen dead is no longer a number.  
**Seam:** this must pay off pre-echoes from Act I / Act II / early Act III. Selma and Eliah must not feel newly inserted.  
**Info:** Selma lost Linn; Eliah carries impossible infant fragments.  
**No-go:** no horror child, no kitsch. Few concrete details.  
**Mirror for Levin:** guilt has bodies; someone signed a paper before someone held a child in wreckage.  
**Motif:** song, child hand, cold glass.  
**End:** Selma names Linn clearly, perhaps for the first time in the book.

### Chapter 15 — Frau Hedigers Jahrestag

**Location / time:** Speisewagen / window, inner approach to Landwasser moment.  
**Surface:** Hediger tells enough: she returns yearly because memory became her prison. Her husband died. She did not repress, but she also did not continue living.  
**Figures:** Hediger, Levin, Marlene, Mancini at edge.  
**Must do:** knowledge alone does not free. Memory must return to life, not only preserve death.  
**Info:** 14 February is fully set. 02:47 is no longer only a glitch, but a wound.  
**Release:** Hediger goes quietly, not as punishment. An object may remain without owner-label.  
**Motif:** police photo, anniversary, glove / ring / object of Heinrich.  
**End:** Levin remains with a memory that is not his and notices his body knows it anyway.

### Chapter 16 — Marlene erinnert sich

**Location / time:** mirror / window / near driver-boundary, but not full Führerstand.  
**Surface:** Marlene recognizes herself: not only photographer, but locomotive driver of the accident train. Camera / reflection / route sound breaks her seal.  
**Figures:** Marlene and Levin central.  
**Must do:** pay off emotional axis. Marlene shows Levin that legal acquittal does not equal inner peace.  
**Info:** she braked 26 seconds too late; she survived and built another life.  
**Mirror for Levin:** she can name professional responsibility; he cannot yet name his.  
**Motif:** camera, braking, mirror, 26 seconds.  
**End:** Marlene says something Levin should apply to himself, but he deflects.

### Chapter 17 — Der letzte Wagen

**Location / time:** rear / last carriage / threshold into Act IV.  
**Surface:** Marlene does not vanish as shock. She goes in recognition. Levin tries to hold her in the world.  
**Figures:** Marlene, Levin, Mancini possibly at end.  
**Must do:** Levin loses the person who could share his perception.  
**Disappearance:** Marlene is not punished; she can move because she has named her truth.  
**Hidden-truth seed:** Marlene asks about Levin's role / where he was. He cannot answer.  
**Motif:** last photo, empty negative, brake sound.  
**End:** Levin is alone with Mancini and the train. The next missing name can only be his.

## ACT IV — 02:47

### Chapter 18 — Das Formular

**Location / time:** service / conductor compartment / record-space overlay.  
**Surface:** Start with Marlene-aftershock. Levin is alone before the form appears. Then he finds the physical truth: old railway trace, maintenance form, checklist, signature, `in Ordnung`, 00:48.  
**Figures:** Levin, Mancini. No long explanation; the room and objects carry the truth.  
**Must do:** final twist lands physically and factually. Levin was the 19-year-old practical worker / trainee who signed the inspection as okay.  
**Full info:** 2008, switch-heater, exhaustion, pressure, signature, legal diffusion, inner repression.  
**No-go:** no therapy speech, no Schaffner lecture, no time-travel mechanism.  
**Motif:** paper, signature, form, metallic splitting.  
**End:** Levin does not remember “everything” as infodump; he remembers one bodily moment: pen, paper, cold fingers, exhaustion, pressure to make it quick.

### Chapter 19 — Die Brücke

**Location / time:** Führerstand / Landwasserviadukt / 02:47 approaching.  
**Surface:** the train moves again — or has always been moving toward this moment. Mancini offers no explanation, only possibility: do nothing, leave, brake.  
**Figures:** Levin, Mancini. Marlene may echo as memory / camera image, not as real rescuing return.  
**Must do:** decision, not explanation.  
**Info:** braking does not change the past; it is a moral action, not a technical fix.  
**Motif:** viaduct, false switch, brake, 02:47, snow.  
**End:** Levin brakes. He knows it changes nothing. He does it anyway.

### Chapter 20 — Schnee, der nach unten fällt

**Location / time:** **Café in Zürich**, after the train, present after recognition.  
**Surface:** the train does not collapse as spectacle. Levin sits with Sarah and speaks for the first time about RhB, the inspection, the form, the accident.  
**Figures:** Levin, Sarah. The absent remain as truth, not ghosts.  
**Must do:** guilt is spoken, not erased. Sarah is not a prize; she listens.  
**Info:** Levin lives on with the past. No magical rescue.  
**Motif payoff:** snow falls downward. Sarah takes his hand.  
**Possible final sentence:** “Draußen begann es zu schneien, aber der Schnee fiel diesmal nach unten.”  
**No-go:** no complete redemption, no retroactive rescue, no ontological explanation of what the train “was.”

## 8. Transition seam rules

These rules were added after the transition audit and must govern beat plans.

### Act I → Act II

Chapter 7 must begin from Tobias residue.

Do not reset the book after the first disappearance.

Working seam:

> First a man is erased from a seat. Then a man is erased from a life.

### Act II → Act III

Anika must not vanish from the text between Jan's Tilgung in Ch7 and her payoff in Ch12.

Minimum echoes across Ch8–11:

- wrong photo framing,
- accidental `wir` corrected to `ich`,
- reaction to Filisur / smoke / platform,
- grief without object.

### Selma / Eliah / Linn pre-echo

Before Ch14, Selma and Eliah need at least two quiet echoes so Ch14 does not feel isolated:

- Eliah hums a line he should not know,
- Selma overreacts to cold glass / child-height mark / counting,
- Levin notices a place held open without explanation,
- Eliah reacts to a brake rhythm or sound before Ch14.

### Act III → Act IV

Chapter 18 must not begin mechanically with the form.

It begins with Marlene-aftershock and Levin's isolation. The document emerges from the absence.

## 9. Open issues for later detail decisions

These do not block Chapter 1, but must be decided before the relevant beat plans.

1. **Robert Marek's material:** What exactly did he know? Why did he not publish? Which trace can Levin find without seeing his own signature too early?
2. **Marlene's disappearance:** How visible is the disappearance? It must be emotionally strong but not melodramatic.
3. **Selma / Eliah:** Whether both are released or Eliah remains as remainder-witness must be fixed before Ch14–15.
4. **Mancini's exact boundary:** He is a witness without direct deed-guilt. His exact limit must be clear before Ch18/19.
5. **Cold coffee late payoff:** Decide whether it disappears, warms, remains cold or loses owner in the final act.
