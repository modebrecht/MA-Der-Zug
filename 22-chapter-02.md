## 0. Chapter function

Chapter 2 must turn the private fracture at the end of Chapter 1 into the first shared external disturbance, without yet proving the train is supernatural.

By the end of the chapter:

- the tunnel / metallic splitting residue from Chapter 1 has echoed into the first interruption;
- the train has come to an unexplained halt in snow / tunnel-adjacent mountain dark;
- power, signal, announcement and passenger confidence have begun to fail;
- Tobias Reck has started taking interpretive authority, but is not yet reduced to a villain type;
- Marlene is established as someone who watches the disturbance more precisely than ordinary passengers;
- Frau Hediger remains too calm, but does not explain;
- Mancini is polite and evasive, not an oracle;
- Levin displays unplaced technical railway knowledge and reacts physically to words like `Störung`, `Wartung`, `Weiche` or `Sichtprüfung`, without understanding why;
- no disappearance / Tilgung has happened yet;
- no Albula 2008 explanation, no inspection form, no signature, no switch-heater reveal;
- the chapter ends with a display stuck at / jumping to `02:47` in a way Levin cannot rationally dismiss.

Chapter 2 should feel like a realistic late-night railway disruption that has begun to rot from the inside.

## 4. Beat details

### Beat 1 — After-silence from Chapter 1

**Location & time:** Wagen 2, immediately after the tunnel sound / failed announcement. Night.  
**POV:** Levin.  
**Dramatic purpose:** Preserve the Chapter 1 cliff-pressure without escalating too fast.  
**Character want:** Levin wants the sound to become ordinary again.  
**Resistance / cost:** The train gives him almost enough normality to doubt himself, but not enough to calm him.  
**What changes by beat end:** The disturbance remains private but has begun to spread through small reactions: Eliah, Marlene, perhaps Hediger.  
**Mystery information gained / withheld / distorted:** Gained: the failed announcement is not immediately corrected. Withheld: no reason. Distorted: tunnel acoustics can still explain too much.  
**Hidden-truth risk:** Low. Do not attach the sound to 2008, brakes, Weiche or accident image.  
**Motif carried:** metallic splitting residue; failed announcement; window mirror fading back into darkness.  
**Sensory anchor:** after-silence, pressure in ears, wheel rhythm trying to return, low tunnel vibration.  
**Seam from previous beat:** Direct continuation from Ch1 final sentence: Levin knows the sound, but refuses the knowledge.  
**Target word count:** 400–500.

### Beat 2 — The train’s motion becomes questionable

**Location & time:** Still in Wagen 2 / transition out of tunnel or into a dark cut of mountain line.  
**POV:** Levin.  
**Dramatic purpose:** Move from private sound to shared physical irregularity.  
**Character want:** Levin wants a measurable sign: speed, station, signal, announcement, anything outside himself.  
**Resistance / cost:** The train’s movement becomes hard to read. It seems to slow, then not slow; the rhythm changes without clear cause.  
**What changes by beat end:** Levin can no longer keep the disturbance purely inside his head.  
**Mystery information gained / withheld / distorted:** Gained: the train is not behaving like a normal smooth tunnel passage. Withheld: whether this is technical fault, weather, signal stop or something else.  
**Hidden-truth risk:** Medium. Levin may know too much about railway rhythm; keep it unplaced and physical, not articulated as professional expertise yet.  
**Motif carried:** movement / stillness ambiguity; sound; cold beginning to enter through carriage seams.  
**Sensory anchor:** wheel rhythm thinning, carriage coupling tug, fluorescent light tremor, a change in floor vibration.  
**Seam from previous beat:** The after-silence creates the need to test whether the train is normal; the changing movement answers badly.  
**Target word count:** 450–600.

### Beat 3 — First real halt

**Location & time:** Albula line, night; exact outside location uncertain.  
**POV:** Levin.  
**Dramatic purpose:** Deliver the chapter’s title event: the train stops.  
**Character want:** Levin wants the halt to have a normal railway cause.  
**Resistance / cost:** The halt arrives with a small echo of the metallic splitting — not a crash, but a brief wrongness in pressure and metal.  
**What changes by beat end:** The train is now objectively stopped or close enough that passengers notice.  
**Mystery information gained / withheld / distorted:** Gained: the halt is real. Withheld: where they are, why the train stopped, whether staff know. Distorted: bad weather remains a plausible explanation.  
**Hidden-truth risk:** Medium. Avoid brake-destiny language. Avoid implying accident replay.  
**Motif carried:** metallic splitting transformed into interruption; emergency light beginning.  
**Sensory anchor:** a soft jolt, metal settling, lights dimming, breath noise louder once motion stops.  
**Seam from previous beat:** The questionable motion finally resolves into stillness.  
**Target word count:** 500–650.

### Beat 4 — Passengers rationalize / react

**Location & time:** Wagen 2 and sightlines into adjacent carriage / corridor.  
**POV:** Levin.  
**Dramatic purpose:** Make the disruption socially real. Let normal passenger psychology push against Levin’s private fear.  
**Character want:** Levin wants other people’s reactions to tell him whether this is ordinary.  
**Resistance / cost:** Reactions scatter: annoyance, fatigue, jokes, phone-checking, complaint, parental overcare, old calm. Nobody reacts exactly as he needs.  
**What changes by beat end:** The group becomes a social pressure field. Levin is not alone, but he is not reassured.  
**Mystery information gained / withheld / distorted:** Gained: phones / signal / announcement are unreliable. Withheld: no impossible rule yet. Distorted: passenger assumptions fill the silence.  
**Hidden-truth risk:** Low. Keep focus on ordinary disruption behaviour.  
**Motif carried:** failed signal, emergency light, cold, Eliah’s sound sensitivity, Hediger’s calm.  
**Sensory anchor:** phone screens searching, coat rustle, emergency lighting flattening faces, heating fan weakening.  
**Seam from previous beat:** The halt forces the passengers to become active.  
**Target word count:** 600–750.

### Beat 5 — Tobias takes interpretive authority

**Location & time:** Wagen 2 / aisle, possibly moving toward Speisewagen threshold but not settling there yet.  
**POV:** Levin.  
**Dramatic purpose:** Begin Tobias’s Act-I social function before Chapter 3 makes Platz 47 concrete.  
**Character want:** Tobias wants control, hierarchy and a language that makes the event manageable. Levin wants that language to work, but distrusts its smoothness.  
**Resistance / cost:** Tobias’s rationality is useful and irritating. He names procedures and responsibilities without truly knowing the situation.  
**What changes by beat end:** Tobias becomes the person others may follow or believe; Levin feels socially smaller for not sharing his certainty.  
**Mystery information gained / withheld / distorted:** Gained: there are plausible technical categories for the halt. Withheld: which, if any, applies. Distorted: Tobias turns uncertainty into administrative confidence.  
**Hidden-truth risk:** Medium. Tobias may use `Störung` or `Zuständigkeit`; do not overdo `Abnahme`, `Protokoll`, `in Ordnung` yet, because Chapter 3 needs room.  
**Motif carried:** process-language lightly; phones without signal; emergency light.  
**Sensory anchor:** Tobias’s coat, phone held like evidence, voice too clear in a stopped carriage.  
**Seam from previous beat:** Passenger discomfort creates a vacuum; Tobias fills it.  
**Target word count:** 500–650.

### Beat 6 — Mancini’s evasive service response

**Location & time:** Corridor between Wagen 2 and service area / Mancini appearing from the train’s direction, not summoned like magic.  
**POV:** Levin.  
**Dramatic purpose:** Give the passengers a staff figure who should explain, but refuses to explain enough.  
**Character want:** Levin wants a real operational answer. Tobias wants confirmation of his authority. Mancini wants to keep order without giving the rule away.  
**Resistance / cost:** Mancini answers in service language that remains technically plausible but emotionally wrong.  
**What changes by beat end:** The disruption is officially acknowledged but not clarified. Mancini’s abnormal calm becomes stronger.  
**Mystery information gained / withheld / distorted:** Gained: there is no immediate onward explanation. Withheld: the true nature of the halt. Distorted: Mancini may use a normal phrase that lands too heavily.  
**Hidden-truth risk:** High. Mancini must not say the train is going to the past, guilt, 2008 or a moment.  
**Motif carried:** ticket / presence logic; service lamp; polite withholding.  
**Sensory anchor:** keys or ticket punch, dim service lamp, damp wool, his voice not rising despite pressure.  
**Seam from previous beat:** Tobias’s authority forces the appearance of official authority; Mancini displaces it without satisfying it.  
**Target word count:** 500–650.

### Beat 7 — Levin’s technical knowledge leaks / end display 02:47

**Location & time:** Wagen 2 / corridor sightline to a display, window or small service indicator; stopped train in snow-dark.  
**POV:** Levin.  
**Dramatic purpose:** Convert the halt into a personal threat for Levin while preserving hidden-truth protection.  
**Character want:** Levin wants one exact reason: signal failure, power issue, weather obstruction, maintenance problem.  
**Resistance / cost:** He catches himself knowing too much about what should happen next: who should call whom, how long an announcement should take, which words staff should or should not use. His body reacts before thought when a term like `Wartung`, `Störung`, `Weiche` or `Sichtprüfung` appears.  
**What changes by beat end:** The reader sees that Levin’s relation to railway procedure is not casual, but Levin still does not decode it. The chapter ends with an objective time wrongness.  
**Mystery information gained / withheld / distorted:** Gained: time / display is now wrong in a harder way than Chapter 1. Withheld: why 02:47 matters. Distorted: Levin may still blame malfunction or fatigue, but the explanation is weakening.  
**Hidden-truth risk:** High. Absolutely no form, signature, 2008, Weichenheizung, `in Ordnung`, or clear memory. His knowledge must be procedural and bodily, not autobiographical.  
**Motif carried:** 02:47; emergency light; snow/cold; railway vocabulary as body trigger.  
**Sensory anchor:** failing display glow, dark glass, snow beyond window, dry mouth, finger numbness, carriage silence after a word.  
**Seam from previous beat:** Mancini’s evasive wording or Tobias’s procedural language triggers Levin’s unplaced knowledge; the display then gives the chapter its impossible punctuation.  
**Target word count:** 650–850.

## 5. Logic & motivation gate

### Character motivation

- **Levin does not immediately investigate aggressively** because the event is still plausibly technical, he fears his own reaction, and he wants external normality to overrule his body.
- **Passengers react with annoyance / fatigue / phone-checking** because a railway halt in winter is inconvenient before it is terrifying.
- **Tobias takes authority** because uncertainty gives him social room to convert confusion into procedure and hierarchy.
- **Marlene watches rather than comforts** because her function is perception-counterpart, not emotional service to Levin.
- **Hediger remains calm** because she carries memory differently, but she does not yet have permission — emotionally or structurally — to explain.
- **Mancini withholds** because his role is threshold / service / witness; he can acknowledge the halt without revealing the train’s rules.

### Obvious-action test

No obvious action is artificially withheld if the prose makes the following clear:

- **Why not call for help?** Phones / signal fail or become unreliable, but this is introduced through passenger behaviour, not author statement.
- **Why not demand answers?** Passengers do demand answers; Tobias and others pressure Mancini. Mancini answers too little.
- **Why not leave the train?** This is not yet an exit-attempt chapter. Doors may remain closed for weather / operational reasons; the full outside-threshold rule belongs later.
- **Why does Levin not confess / remember?** Nothing has yet given him a full autobiographical link. The triggers remain bodily and procedural.
- **Why does Hediger not explain?** She is observant and wounded, not an exposition machine; at this stage she has no reason to trust Levin with the whole pattern.
- **Why does Mancini not explain?** Explanation would violate his function and the train’s withholding logic. He keeps order, not clarity.

- Questionable movement resolves into halt.
