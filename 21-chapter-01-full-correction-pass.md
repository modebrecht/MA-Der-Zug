## 1. Overall verdict

Chapter 1 is ready as a complete first draft.

It successfully does what the chapter must do:

- establishes Levin in the Engadin / Samedan departure context, not Zürich;
- gives him a concrete present-world reason to reach Chur: Sarah;
- moves from open platform into closed train world;
- introduces the ensemble through behavior rather than a cast list;
- plants Mancini as subtly wrong without making him an oracle;
- plants `02:47` exactly once;
- plants Levin’s old railway/body memory without exposing the 2008 truth;
- ends on the metallic-splitting sound and private recognition without explanation.

No structural rewrite is required before continuing to Chapter 2 planning.

## 2. Logic audit

### Route / geography

Pass.

Levin starts in Samedan after a professional appointment and boards toward Chur. This satisfies the Albula route logic and avoids the earlier Zürich → Chur route problem.

### Character motivation

Pass.

Levin’s actions are plausible:

- he waits because he has to reach Chur;
- he answers Sarah because she matters and because she told him not to come;
- he observes because he is tired, controlled and professionally detail-oriented;
- he rationalizes the oddities because nothing impossible has objectively happened yet.

No obvious-action problem appears. He has no reason yet to leave the train, interrogate Mancini, challenge the other passengers, or investigate the route.

### Beat causality

Pass.

The beat chain is clean:

1. professional fatigue / Samedan platform;
2. Sarah as emotional pressure;
3. boarding and closed train world;
4. ensemble revealed through behavior;
5. Mancini ticket/passenger pass;
6. one-time `02:47` flicker;
7. railway-adjacent body memory begins and aborts;
8. tunnel sound pays the chapter pressure.

This produces escalation without jumping too fast into overt mystery.

## 4. Figure audit

### Levin

Pass.

He is controlled, avoidant, observant and tired. His body reacts before his conscious mind. This is consistent with the intended protagonist: a man whose life is organized around not looking directly at the central wound.

### Sarah

Pass.

She works as present-world anchor, not melodrama.

The hand seed is strong but restrained. Her condition is present without being named or explained. This protects her from becoming a symbolic illness device.

### Marlene

Pass.

Her camera and her watching function well. She is not romanticized and not revealed too early. Her reflected eye contact in the tunnel correctly positions her as a future perception-counterpart.

### Tobias / Reck

Pass with watch.

His process-language is strong and immediately readable as responsibility-avoidance. This is useful because he is the first hard Tilgung candidate later.

Watch for later: avoid making him a caricature. In Chapter 2/3 he should still have plausible self-justification, not just villain-office language.

### Frau Hediger

Pass.

Her precision is distinctive. The line about Zugluft is excellent because it makes her competent without turning her into exposition. Her “same route” exchange with Mancini is a controlled hint, not a lore dump.

### Anika / Jan

Pass.

Private tension and the phone-photo seed are sufficient for Chapter 1. They are visible but not over-explained.

### Selma / Eliah

Pass.

Eliah’s sound sensitivity is planted early and pays into Beat 8. Selma’s overcare is present but already complicated by Mancini’s correction. Good setup for later without forcing the Linn wound early.

### Robert

Pass.

Correctly only a rear-glimpse: sleeping man, notebook, no information yet.

### Mancini

Pass.

He is subtly wrong but still deniable:

- knows names through tickets;
- holds Levin’s name a beat too long;
- watches presence rather than luggage;
- says “ich bin im Zug,” which is ordinary but slightly too exact.

No oracle language appears.

### Minor polish watchlist for later prose pass

These do not block moving forward:

1. **Beat 4: Reck language is very clear.**  
   Keep it, but do not add more of the same in Chapter 2 or he may become too obviously “the system-guilt man.”

2. **Beat 4: phrase `palmennah` is unusual.**  
   Consider replacing later with a simpler phrase such as `nah an ihrer Hand`, unless the oddness is intentional.

3. **Beat 1/3: cold/glass imagery repeats productively.**  
   Continue, but in Chapter 2 vary the sensory channel: more sound / pressure / stillness, less only cold glass.

4. **Beat-local correction sections make the file heavy.**  
   For drafting they are useful. Before manuscript export, prose should be extracted or correction notes moved out.
